## Biography

Andrew Morton is a veteran Linux kernel developer who maintains the -mm tree (Andrew's tree), a testing ground for new kernel patches before they reach the mainline. He has been a central figure in kernel development for over two decades.

## Key Contributions

- Maintains the -mm kernel tree
- Memory management subsystem
- Patch review and integration
- Mentoring new kernel developers

## Impact

Andrew's -mm tree serves as a crucial staging area for experimental kernel features. His careful review process and commitment to code quality have helped maintain the kernel's reputation for stability despite rapid development.
